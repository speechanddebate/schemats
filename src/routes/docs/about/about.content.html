Goals

The goal of Tabroom.com is to provide a tournament management system to debate and speech tournaments worldwide, for as many formats of debate as possible. Tabroom is a project owned and operated by the National Speech & Debate Association.

The Tabroom system handles public pairings, enables judges to enter decisions and scores online, and publishes and track results all online. Tabroom also supports speech events, debate events including Worlds Schools and Congressional Debate, and Mock Trial.

If you have a format of debate that isn't on our list, please let us know at help@tabroom.com. Please also test your format if you do not know that Tabroom has run your format or rules successfully before.

History

The Tabroom system began as two projects; the CAT (Computer Assisted Tab) and debateresults.com, created by Professor Jon Bruschke at California State University at Fullerton, and Tabroom.com, begun as a side project by Chris Palmer. It also owes a debt of inspiration, ideas and gratitude to Professor Rich Edwards at Baylor University's foundational TRPC and TRIE-PC programs.

Need Help?

If you're a student, coach, or parent with a question about a particular tournament, email them directly — they're more likely to help. Contact information can usually be found in the tournament invitation or main website.

If you're having trouble with the Tabroom software itself, visit:

Tabroom Documentation

If those don't work or your question involves something private (e.g. personal student info, access needs), email us at help@tabroom.com. Please include as much detail as possible about the tournament, event, entry, school, or round affected, as well as the URL of the page you're having trouble with.

To run a tournament on Tabroom, log in and hit "Request a tournament" on the right hand toolbar. No pre-notification is required to register or tabulate a tournament on Tabroom.
